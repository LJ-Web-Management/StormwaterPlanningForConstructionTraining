Overview

Why Stormwater Training Matters on Construction Sites

Construction General Permit coverage requires trained personnel handling two different jobs: preparing a defensible SWPPP and correctly implementing it in the field. Getting either one wrong is one of the most common sources of NPDES permit violations on active sites.

📋

Two Roles, Covered Separately

Distinct training for the person who writes the SWPPP and the person who implements and inspects it in the field, so each role gets the depth it actually needs.

Curriculum

What Each Course Covers

Focused, practical modules broken out by role.

QSP Practitioner

Field Implementation Track

  1. 01

    SWPPP Fundamentals & Regulatory Framework

    Structure and goal of a SWPPP, and the Clean Water Act / NPDES permitting system it operates under.

  2. 02

    Role of the Qualified SWPPP Practitioner

    Professional duties and responsibilities of a QSP during active construction activities.

  3. 03

    Recognizing Stormwater Hazards

    Pollution discharges, sediment transport, and erosion risks common on construction sites.

  4. 04

    BMP Selection & Installation

    Choosing, installing, and maintaining the correct Best Management Practices for site conditions.

  5. 05

    Site Inspections & Reporting

    Performing inspections, documenting findings, and recommending corrective actions on deficiencies.

  6. 06

    Pollution Prevention in the Field

    Safe work practices near storm drains and water bodies, chemical storage, waste management, and spill response.

  7. 07

    Site Conditions & Best Professional Judgment

    Interpreting site maps, soil, and weather to forecast problems, and coordinating with owners, contractors, and designers.

SWPPP Preparer

Plan Preparation Track

  1. 01

    SWPPP Fundamentals & Key Elements

    Defining a Stormwater Pollution Prevention Plan and its required elements.

  2. 02

    Clean Water Act & CGP Requirements

    Section 402, the NPDES program, and Construction General Permit coverage and documentation rules.

  3. 03

    Role of the Qualified SWPPP Preparer

    Responsibilities and certification statements tied to preparing a permit-ready SWPPP.

  4. 04

    Site Hazard & Risk Assessment

    Drainage patterns, pollutant sources, and site evaluation for plan development.

  5. 05

    BMP Selection & Design Criteria

    Erosion control, sediment control, pollution prevention, and Best Professional Judgment in plan preparation.

  6. 06

    Monitoring & Numeric Limits

    Numeric Action Levels, Numeric Effluent Limits, and when sampling results require a SWPPP revision.

  7. 07

    Documentation, Updates & Final Stabilization

    Common preparation errors, required SWPPP updates, and final stabilization / vegetation criteria.
